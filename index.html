The Next Olympic Events

The Olympic Games are a pleasant time when we can all get together and root for our country in sports that we may or may not even know the rules for. So, why not add to the fun by adding even more of them? Olympic organizers have invited nine sports to make their case for inclusion at the 2028 Games in Los Angeles, which you can learn more about below.

Baseball/Softball

Baseball/Softball

Baseball is a classic American sport, but it hasn't caught on as an Olympic mainstay. It debuted at the Olympics in St. Louis in 1904, though only as an exhibition. It remained a demonstration sport with a constantly changing format until 1992, when it became an official part of the Olympic program. Baseball and softball remained through 2008, but after years of Major League Baseball denying its players from competing, it was dropped from the 2012 and 2016 Games. It came back in 2020 but will not be in the 2024 Paris Games. Due to its popularity in the U.S., it's expected to be included in the 2028 program.

Cricket

Cricket

If cricket were included in the 2028 Games, it would technically not be a first. In 1900, Great Britain and France competed for the cricket gold medal, but they were the only two. Since then, cricket has been absent from the Olympics, but its supporters have made a push to include it in 2028. It's highly popular in and around India. The sport itself is similar to baseball, but the ball is bowled to the batter. It includes several other key rules that would be too long to list here, so click the link above to learn more.

Squash

Squash

Every attempt the World Squash Federation has made to get into the Olympics has been... well, squashed. However, it's once again on the docket to make its Olympic push. If you get past the funky name, squash can be an entertaining watch. It's like if tennis players played on a court with one side folded up to a wall, with the competitors smashing the ball against that wall until the other hits it out of bounds or fails to hit it.

Motorsport

Motorsport

Racecar driving in the Olympics? It wouldn't be the first time. Way back in 1900, motorsport made its only appearance in the Games. The cars may have changed since then, but the draw is still the same: vehicles racing against each other at ridiculous speeds. Motorsport includes a variety of disciplines, but the one expected to make it to the Olympics is karting.

Lacrosse

Lacrosse

Lacrosse is a physical game where two teams of ten players suit up in pads and helmets and attempt to outscore the other. They use a long stick with a net at the end to corral the ball and shoot it into the net (and occasionally to whack another player). It was included in the 1904 and 1908 Olympic Games, but has been absent except as an occasional demonstration event. With the sort's massive popularity in the U.S., 2028 would make for a good time for lacrosse to make its comeback.

Kickboxing

Kickboxing

This combat sport has never been a part of the Olympic Games. The violent and bloody nature of it has made the Olympic Committee wary to include it for an international audience, but with the meteoric rise in popularity of mixed martial arts, especially in the U.S., it's possible kickboxing makes it in. For the uninitiated, kickboxing is exactly what it sounds like: traditional hand-to-hand boxing with a variety of kicks for fighters to use at their disposal.

Breaking

Breaking

Breaking, or breakdancing, is the only one of these sports that has been confirmed for the 2024 Games in Paris, France. Of all these, breakdancing may seem like the most... unlike a sport. Most people have seen street breakdancers spinning like a top on their head and hands to the amazement of crowds, but in the Olympics, these performers will be battling head-to-head. The competitors will be scored on their creativity, personality, technique, variety, performativity and musciality.

Flag Football

Flag Football

This Olympic bid is being pushed by the National Football League. It's an attempt to grow the sport to an international audience, at least according to the league. If flag football were to be added any year, it would be 2028, as the city of Los Angeles alone has two professional football teams.

Karate

Karate

Karate actually made its Olympic debut with both men's and women's divisions in the 2020 Games in Tokyo, but after some backlash, it was removed from the 2024 program. It might be a tough sell to the committee given it's a combat sport, but that remains to be seen. Karate is a classic form of martial arts, with two categories put in Olympic competition. Kumite is semi-contact and judged on points, while kata is full contact and graded on power.
